How VeriShot creates a Verified Proof

See exactly how VeriShot creates a verified proof photo with timestamp, GPS coordinates, and a Proof ID—then stores, searches, maps, and shares it.

What’s inside a proof?

Every VeriShot proof is an image that includes a readable overlay with the key facts a client needs:

  • Proof ID — a short identifier to reference the verification
  • Timestamp — the time the proof was captured
  • GPS coordinates — latitude and longitude
  • Status — Verified / Unverified (shown clearly in the stamp)

The 5-step workflow

  1. Open Camera and confirm Location Active.
  2. Take Photo — capture the scene in one tap.
  3. Save as Proof — VeriShot generates a Proof ID and stamps the image.
  4. Browse or Search in the Proof Gallery (by ID or date).
  5. Share the message‑ready proof to clients, teams, or your own records.

Map view

VeriShot can show proofs pinned by location so you can quickly validate job sites, routes, and field activity.

Sharing

When you share a proof, recipients can immediately see the stamped facts (ID, time, location). You can share via Messages, Mail, AirDrop, or any supported iOS share target.

Notes on accuracy

GPS coordinates come from your device’s location services. For best results, capture proofs with a strong GPS signal. Always review the stamp before sending if your workflow requires strict precision.
